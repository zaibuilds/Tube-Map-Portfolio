    • Problem:

    Users struggle to find reliable skincare and information advice directly from one source alongside struggling to maintain consistency in a skincare routine

    Role:

    UX Researcher, UI Designer

    Project Duration:

    10 Days

    Research Tools:

    Google Meets, Whimsical

    Figma


    Intro:

    Oracle was an idea I had just under a year ago that never came to fruition. I was struggling with acne at the time and thought an app helping to find reliable skincare advice and track my skincare routine would be of great help. This year (2021) I decided to develop this idea.

    Process

    For the project I decided to utilise the human centred design approach; Empathise, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test.

    Research

    My initial hope for this project was to find that 75% of users would use my product. After conducting interviews with 20 participants, I discovered that 90% of users interviewed would be interested in a social media app for skincare lovers.

    Alongside completing this feasibility study I delved deeper into analysing userâ€™s habits. I chose to focus on 4 main areas of interest.


    Issues users faced when purchasing skincare products online or otherwise

    Where users looked for skincare product information

    Where users looked for skincare advice

    Did users use social media to find skincare product recommendations? If yes, what did they use?


    Issues users faced when purchasing skincare products online or otherwise

    13 users reported 1 or more issues with purchasing skincare

    5 users reported stock availability as being an issue when purchasing skincare products online

    2 users reported being unable to test products in person as being an issue with purchasing skincare online, 1 of these 2 users said this was especially an issue when purchasing sunscreen due to white cast

    2 users reported being unsure of how to implement a product in a routine as being an issue


    Other issues users reported were products not living up to expectations, inconsistent reviews, product efficacy and finding products suitable for their skin type.


    Where users looked for skincare product information

    12 users reported usually looking for skincare product information on YouTube

    8 users reported usually looking for skincare product information on Google

    4 users reported usually looking for skincare product information on Instagram

    3 users reported usually looking for skincare product information on a product description

    2 users reported usually looking for skincare product information on Twitter

    2 users reported usually looking for skincare product information on Reddit

    1 user reported using word of mouth


    Where users looked for skincare advice

    11 users reported looking for skincare advice on YouTube

    5 users reported looking for skincare advice on Google

    3 users reported looking for skincare advice on Twitter

    2 users reported looking for skincare advice on Instagram

    2 users reported looking for skincare advice on Reddit

    Other users reported using word of mouth or asking friends familiar with skincare for advice.


    Did users use social media to find skincare product recommendations? If yes, what did they use?

    15 users stated that they used social media to find skincare product recommendations

    8 users reported using Twitter to find product recommendations

    8 users reported using YouTube to find product recommendations

    8 users reported using Instagram to find product recommendations

    2 users reported using Reddit to find product recommendations

    1 user reported using TikTok


    Alongside collecting data on user habits, I also received some valuable data regarding what Oracle should contain. One of my initial ideas was to implement tracking and also data insights, similar to Flo, within the app to aid users with maintaining consistency in their skincare routine as well as track skin issues. 2 participants echoed implementing these features.

    Additionally, in regards to implementing a tracking feature, 16 users said an app with this feature would benefit them.

    Ideation

    For my ideation, I decided to create mind maps detailing which features Oracle could potentially incorporate. Alongside this I also created some low fidelity sketches of a few of the screens.

    Alongside this, I also created user flows for some of the key journeys.

    Design

    After deciding which features to include and completing my low fidelity sketches, I created some mid fidelity wireframes in Figma.

    After creating mid-fidelity wireframes, I decided to make a series of style guides and then create my initial high-fidelity wireframe.

    I decided to use Montserrat as it is a Sans-serif font and accessible for neurodiverse users and those with visual impairments.

    The colour palette I used is largely monochrome; resulting in it being gender neutral. This results in a larger potential market as opposed to if the colour palette was more feminine.

    One design decision I was uncertain of was the design of the navigation bar. In order to make a decision I conducted an A/B test, as can be seen below. After making this decision I then completed the remainder of the high-fidelity wireframe.

    What I learned:

    The value of collecting both quantitative and qualitative data when conducting research, how to conduct user interviews and also how to conduct usability testing.


    What I did:

    * Implemented features influenced by insights from data that I collected; such as user verification, a social feed and the tracking feature
