    • Intro

    KIRAA is a clothing rental platform catered towards B2C clients as well as C2C consumers.

    Role

    Lead Product Designer

    Responsibilities

    During my time at KIRAA I acted as the Lead Product Designer; taking charge of product and project management, marketing strategy, UX research and UI design.

    Process

    For this project I utilised the Human-Centred Design Process during every stage.

    Brand Design

    At KIRAA I took ownership of the brand design prior to taking on the user interface.

    I used Affinity Designer to create various artefacts including a logo, brand guidelines, colour palette and brand book.

    These can be seen below:

    For the logo I chose to use a serif font (Groucho BT) and tweaked this using Affinity Designer, joining together the first three characters of the brand name.


    UX Research

    Alongside creating KIRAA’s branding I also conducted UX research to find product market fit and understand user’s attitudes towards both fast fashion and clothing rental, I did this via a survey using Google Forms and also conducted interviews with focus groups.

    The findings of this survey can be seen below.

    UI Design

    Following the completion of the initial UX research, I began to work on KIRAA’s user interface.

    I began by creating the design system and then proceeded to work on the mobile user interface and then the desktop.

    Initially; I wanted to explore different design styles to find one which aligned with KIRAA’s branding.

    I explored neubrutalism initially and then moved onto neumorphism, I did this by creating item cards in both styles, these can be seen below:

    Following this, I began to work on the rest of the design system.

    I focused on creating reusable and responsive components by using a combination of variants as well as autolayout; these component sets can be seen below:

    Once the design system was complete I began to work on the mobile UI.

    I initially completed mid-fidelity wireframes, as can be seen below.

    Following this, I created high fidelity wireframes.

    One page I initially struggled with designing was the home page.

    This was due to the fact that I needed the search bar functionality, however I was unsure if I wanted the homepage and the search function to be separate on mobile.
